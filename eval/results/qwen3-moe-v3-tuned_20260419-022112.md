**Gold:** ['expert']
**Response** (3062 tokens, 3.28s, 933.8 tok/s):

```
An Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) model is a type of artificial intelligence model that consists of multiple smaller models, or "experts," each specialized in handling a specific subset of tasks or data. These experts are combined in a way that allows the model to dynamically select the most appropriate expert(s) for a given input, based on the model's internal routing mechanism. This approach is inspired by the way humans can draw on different areas of expertise to solve complex problems.

One reason an MoE model is appealing compared to a dense model of similar quality is efficiency. MoE models can be more computationally efficient because they only activate a subset of their experts for any given input, rather than activating all components of a dense model. This can lead to reduced energy consumption and faster inference times, making MoE models particularly suitable for deployment on resource-constrained devices or in scenarios where real-time performance is critical.
```
